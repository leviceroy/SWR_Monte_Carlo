  Portfolio 3 fees: 0.1560% = $156K total cost

  Why gold is expensive:
  - GLD expense ratio: 0.40%
  - 20% allocation √ó 0.40% = 0.08% of total portfolio
  - Over 50 years: ~$200K in gold fees alone

  2. International Reduces Returns (Historically)

  Portfolio 1 (no international) vs Portfolio 2 (26% international):
  - Portfolio 1: 8.0% expected return
  - Portfolio 2: 7.0% expected return (-1%)
  - Why? VXUS historically returns ~8% vs VTI ~10%

  But: International provides diversification insurance
  - If US underperforms next 50 years (like it did 2000s)
  - International might outperform
  - No one knows the future!

  3. Three-Fund Has Higher Drawdowns

  Why Portfolio 2 has 41.6% drawdown vs Portfolio 1's 35%:
  - Portfolio 1: 20% SGOV (ultra-stable treasuries)
  - Portfolio 2: 20% BND (total bond market, more volatile)
  - SGOV SD: 2% vs BND SD: 3%
  - Plus Portfolio 2 has 80% equities vs 80% but different mix

  Tradeoff:
  - Higher drawdowns = more volatility = higher long-term returns
  - Lower fees offset this somewhat

  4. Gold is Insurance, Not Investment

  Golden Butterfly (Portfolio 3) performance:
  - Lower median than others
  - But performs BETTER in:
    - High inflation (gold shines)
    - Stock/bond correlation increases
    - "Everything crashes" scenarios

  Cost of insurance:
  - Give up ~1-2% annual returns
  - Pay 0.40% expense ratio on 20% of portfolio
  - Worth it? Depends on your fear level
